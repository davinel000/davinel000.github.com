## Introductory Description

After (Fleeting Retention) is an immersive audiovisual installation exploring the ephemerality of memory through powerful LED flashes and generative ambient sound. A modified Kodak Carousel slide projector displays semicontextualized black-and-white negatives (1950s-1970s), triggering fleeting afterimages in the viewer's perception. The random sequencing (5-40 seconds) and short bursts of light (0.1-2 seconds) accentuate the instability of recollection, reinforcing the theme of "new energies" as impermanent and ever-shifting.

## Description and Core Elements

### Kodak Carousel Projector (Wide-Angle Lens)

Custom-fitted with an ultra-bright 100-200 W LED + additional heat sink, controlled via an ESP32-based microcontroller.

Random intervals of flash and slide switching (5-20 seconds), synced with a generative realtime ambient soundtrack (Bespoke Synth).

Contains a 81-slide carousel loaded with archival black-and-white negatives of high contrast (curated locally contextualized collection of old photos).

### Generative Ambient Composition

Real-time drone-based score produced by an Intel NUC running Bespoke Synth and additional control in TouchDesigner/Chataigne (via OSC/MIDI - Serial).

Audio output uses a Behringer UMC1820 interface and two 150W bi-amped loudspeakers (WORK WPL IN-A). Being considered as a minimal setup, it can be extended with locally present audio system (adding subwoofers).

### Low-level Red Light

Low-level dimmed neutral scattered red light sources for safe navigation - set of rechargeable compact flashlights with red LED or red light filter (needed to be included in budget).

### Interactive Sensory Experience

Flashes create strong afterimages in a darkened room.

Emphasizes the tension between high-intensity stimuli and slow sonic droning, reflecting on the fleeting nature of memory.

## Dimensions / Duration

- Approx. installation footprint: 3.5 m wide x 3.5 m deep
- Recommended space: 12 x 12 m or larger to allow comfortable visitor flow and partial seating (e.g. beanbags)
- Projection distance: ideally centered on a wall or surface, with enough distance to achieve large-scale projection (floor-to-ceiling if possible, depending on the ceiling height)

## Packed Dimensions & Weight

- 1 Eurobox (40 x 60 x 40 cm), approx. 15 kg (projector, controllers, cables)
- 2 loudspeakers (approx. 12 kg each), plus stands in a separate bag
- 1 tripod or stand for projector (approx. 6 kg, up to 120 cm long)

## Operation Duration

- Sound and flashes run continuously during opening hours
- Each flash event: 0.1-2 seconds, strobe patterns possible
- Slide transitions occur unpredictably (20-60 second intervals)

## Spatial Requirements

- Minimum room size: 400 x 400 cm (16 m2) to accommodate all elements
- Alternative arrangements for non-standard rooms are possible but subject to discussion
- Preferred space: a separated and enclosed room is needed for an intimate and focused interaction
- Dark environment: a fully dark room is crucial
- Use a double curtain or similar light-trap method to ensure minimal ambient light

### Visitor Navigation

- Subdued red light recommended outside the entrance or in a small corridor to allow eyes to adapt
- In-room luminous tape or minimal LED floor markers to guide visitors safely

### Flooring

- Stable and free of reflective surfaces to prevent light scatter

### Acoustic Space

- The installation uses mid-to-high volume sound
- Position it so that external noise or other artworks do not conflict

### Environmental Conditions

- The space should be free from high humidity, dirt, and dust to ensure the operation of 12v non-waterproof electronic equipment connected to a 220v power supply

## Technical Requirements (Provided by Exhibition / Venue)

### Power Supply

- 220v / 50Hz / approx. 3A (Schuko plug)
- Dark-colored power strip with at least 6 outlets (projector LED, microcontroller, audio interface, 2 loudspeakers, and PC)
- Distance to the nearest electrical source: up to approx. 4 m from the main setup

### Blackout Materials

- Enough black Molton or equivalent to fully darken windows, doors, or other light sources
- Basic black curtains or double-layer drapes for the entrance to maintain a dark interior

### Supplementary Light

- One or two low-intensity red LED fixtures or small red-filter lamps outside or near the entrance for safe orientation

### Cable Management

- Gaffer tape or cable covers to secure wires on the floor and avoid tripping hazards

### Safety Materials

- Safety navigation tape, capable to stick to the floor and curtains
- Printed flashing hazard notice

### Optional Local Archival Negatives

- If available, the venue may supply additional local negatives to expand content
- Must be standard 35mm or 6x6 frames cut and mounted in slides

## Sound

The system produces a mid-to-high volume generative drone-ambient track with random accent spikes tied to flash events.

Frequency range includes low frequencies - viewers may feel mild bass vibrations.

The recommended isolated or acoustically neutral space is to prevent noise bleed to or from adjacent works.

## Lighting

- Room lighting: must remain off or very minimal
- Red lamp for exit: a small red lamp or LED strip can be placed near the exit for safety
- No additional white light: any overhead lighting must be turned off or dimmed to zero

## Safety of the Artwork On-site

### Flash Warnings

- Bright flashes every 20-60 seconds may pose risks for photosensitive visitors
- A sign at the entrance is required
- This sign should also warn of sudden darkness and strobing

### Visitor Orientation

- Luminous tape or low-level floor guidance helps visitors move inside the dark space
- A small aisle or buffer zone ensures nobody trips over the projector stand or cables
- Assistance on-site: very important, controlling access on site

### Equipment Stability

- Projector firmly fixed on its stand
- Speakers on stable stands with an appropriate base
- If possible, a mediator or staff member should periodically check the area

### Power and Heat Management

- The LED module requires a heat sink and ventilation
- Keep the area ventilated but dark
- The PC and audio interface must be placed away from foot traffic, preferably in a corner or behind a partition

## Transport Requirements

- One Eurobox (40 x 60 x 40 cm, approx. 15 kg)
- Two loudspeakers (approx. 12 kg each) + stands in separate bags, or a stereo system provided on site
- One tripod bag (approx. 6 kg, approx. 120 cm length)

The artwork is fragile. Handle with care to avoid damaging the projector lens, LED assembly, and archival slides.

## Setup / Installation

### Room Preparation

- Achieve full blackout using Molton or other materials
- Ensure a double curtain or door flap for entrances/exits

### Positioning and Cabling

- Place the projector stand at the optimal distance for large-scale projection
- Secure the audio system to each side of the space
- Run cables (power, audio, USB) neatly taped down

### Equipment Power-Up Sequence

- Turn on computer (Intel NUC), then audio interface, then speakers
- Microcontroller / Arduino starts automatically, receiving triggers from the generative software
- A test run of flash intervals and sound calibration is recommended

### Focusing and Flash Testing

- Focus the projector lens in darkness, verifying images are sharp enough for ephemeral recognition
- Confirm strobe frequencies and brightness levels are aligned with the intended concept

## Maintenance and Daily Operation

- Power up the NUC, audio interface, and loudspeakers in correct sequence
- The microcontroller will automatically run
- Check slides occasionally to ensure no jam in the carousel and all slides remain secure
- Clean any dust or debris from lens and stands

## Contact & Additional Notes
