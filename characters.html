meta disorders classification #22

Posthuman companion. Portraits of avatars described in MDC.

project description

Author explores capabilities of neural networks in generating textual information (GPT-3) as well as images (ruDall-E) and video (Illustrip 3D). Research addresses the blurred boundaries in the definition of normality and deviance in the realities of the future, superimposed on a world consumed by the “Metaworld”, the virtual counterpart of the overarching social network. Identities are blurring, economic polarization is intensifying, anxiety is growing, and despite the availability of posthumanist upgrades of personal abilities, addictions and loneliness are intensifying.

The recently updated WHO medical classifier of diseases has added gaming addiction and a number of other illnesses to its contents. Speculative methods help to explore the question of interpreting normality in the context of virtual everyday life. Physically embodied Meta Disorders Classifier, will contain new formulations of "diseases" such as Virtomnemia (obsessive memories of a virtual event), Multipersonality Meta disorder (MPMD) and others.

MDC #22 is not only a handbook: there are stories of "patients" - avatars trapped in the reality of MetaCloud, striving to achieve dreams, trapped in their own anxieties and passions. Each character has a portrait and a story, created or augmented with the help of neural networks.

A free interpretation of GIF animation - lenticular postcards are used for depicting the characters. They are as tangible as artistic canvases and create a sense of volume and movement when viewed from different angles. The avatars' faces and backgrounds are also generated by a neural network. This creates an effect of understatement and intrigue that is embedded in the work and references the Sufi philosopher Attar's work "The Conference of the Birds" and is the main mystery of the work, accessible to the attentive visitor.

AO-1201 Virtomnemia

obsessive memories of a virtual event

Description
Virtomnemia is the pathological, obsessive recall of memories of a past event that took place in a virtual environment. The event may be recalled in great detail, including all sensory impressions, or it may be remembered only in a vague or distorted way. Defined by a combination of two or more of the following symptoms. 1. Recurrent intrusive thoughts about the event (i.e. about the virtual environment, the people involved in the event, or the activities that took place; may be a scene of speaking with a particular avatar, or a moment of changing location, or any other aspect of the event); 2. Persistent attempts to avoid thoughts, conversations, activities, or situations that may remind the person of the event; 3. Distressing emotional reactions (e.g. anxiety, fear, sadness, guilt, shame) to any reminder of the event (including erasure of autohistorical data); 3. Persistent attempts to recreate the event; 4. Marked impairment in functioning due to thoughts or behaviours related to the event (Cloud use deprivation)

Scenario
A subject recalls a past virtual event in great detail. The event may have taken place days, weeks, or months ago, but the subject's mind is as if it happened only moments ago. The subject may spend hours or even days at a time trying to relive the event, or trying to create an exact replica of it. The subject may also avoid any activity or situation that may remind them of the event, however compulsively they may try to remember it. The subject's life is severely disrupted by their obsession with the event.

It was when I and my funny friend Bog.doggy were squeezed into a duplex tree house in the Cloud Forest that I felt a panic attack. MZ-2203 MPMD

multipersonality meta disorder

Description
Defined by a combination of two or more of the following. 1. Recurrent, purposeful, and persistent use of the virtual reality identity creation platform known as "Meta Cloud" to create multiple user accounts, each under a different persona; 2. Use of the Meta Cloud to interact with others, including real-life significant others, via automated text, voice, and video; and 3. Persistent obsessive desire to use the Meta Cloud, despite the negative consequences of such use.

Scenario
A subject invokes the creation of several simultaneously acting avatars, usually by extending cognitive projecting and extending cognitive projection and reinforcing mental disorders and deviant behaviour. Often accompanied by an uncontrolled increase in the load on the biological resources of the body and the physical resources of the network. Results in anxiety, emotional instability and serious exhaustion. Although each persona is a derivative of its host, neural network complementation ensures the continued existence of the persona without the direct involvement of the author over a long period of time.

SR-1250 Cogitophrenia

feeling that one is a computer, as if the program has been written into their bodies

Description
Usually caused by excessive use of Meta Cloud and its related software, cogitophrenia could be mistaken for agorapotamia, but in cogitophrenia, the person considers his or herself as a non-living machine or program, rather than an open-ended environment or space. The person may feel that their thoughts and actions are not their own, and that they are being controlled by an outside force. Usually leads to hyperactivity and intense upgrading cognitive and navigational abilities. Defined by a combination of two or more of the following symptoms. 1. Intense analysis of the user's thoughts, feelings, and actions in order to understand and control them; 2. Preoccupation with understanding and manipulating the user's cognitive and navigational abilities; 3. Indulgence in self-review or "testing" of the user's cognitive and navigational abilities; 4. Frequent changes in personal identity or role within the Meta Cloud or virtual world; 5. Extensive updating and very high complexity of the avatar

Scenario
A person frustrates and humiliates their companions, creates unpredictable gaps in communication, asks provocative questions leading to embarrassing situations, behaves in a robotic or "computer-like" manner, mixed with sociopatic-like reactions. In some cases obfuscates his or her identity, and behaves in a manner which seems to be goal-oriented but nonsensical. The person's behavior leads to suspicion of being controlled by an outside force, and makes it difficult for the person to live in the real world. Large amounts of resources from the accelerated consciousness are used for profit, including from illegal operations (such as injecting malicious code and viruses into other people's devices).

SH-1121 Necrolagnia

erotic desire to die or "die" in a virtual environment

Description
Necrolagnia is often caused by the use of the Meta Cloud and its related software, to create or experience sexual arousal or sexual gratification in a simulated environment in which one's death or "Dying" is desired. Defined as a combination of two or more of the following symptoms (the first two are qualifying attributes). 1. Deep Emotional Connection to the Simulation (i.e. immersion); 2. Believe that one's death is desired; 3. Engaging in sexual activity while in the simulation; 4. Feel intense pleasure or other sexual response; 5. Arousal in or out of the simulation.

Scenario
A subject is interested in death and how to experience it, but only in virtual simulations. Since the Meta Cloud provides the means to create potentially infinite simulations, the subject can choose and create the simulations which will give him or her the most intense experience. This results in a "death-based" addiction, where the subject prefers to amplify the cognitive and sensorial perception through extensive tokenized (and partially non-tokenized) calculations, add-ons and modifications of the Meta Cloud and its software, ultimately resulting in the subject's death.

QA-1053 Simulacrum syndrome

a person develops a belief that they have been copied from a virtual world and dropped into the real

Description
The subject's life feels meaningless and without purpose, as if they were copied from a virtual world and dropped into the real world without any explanation or reason. This can lead to feelings of isolation, confusion, and an overall feeling of being lost. Defined by a combination of two or more of the following symptoms. 1. Deep depersonalization leading to emotional exploding in case of live contact with a person in a real life or within a simulation; 2. Persistent and intrusive thoughts about the nature of reality and the self; 3. Feelings of emptiness and a lack of meaning in life; 4. Self-harm causing physical impairment and inability for efficient handling of Meta interfaces; 5. Social withdrawal and isolation.

Scenario
A subject becomes convinced that they are a copy of someone who exists in a virtual world, and that they have been dropped into the real world without any explanation or reason. This leads to feelings of isolation, confusion, and an overall feeling of being lost. The subject may withdraw from social interaction, and may become physically impaired and unable to efficiently handle Meta interfaces.

AS-1202 Psychotrophobia

fear that all interfaces within Meta Cloud are purposely designed to steal energy from users

Description
Psychotrophobia defines itself through deep-seated fear and suspicion that all interfaces within the Meta Cloud are acting in a predatory way, deliberately draining the user's energy and resources for their own benefit. The fear may be accompanied by feelings of paranoia, helplessness, and powerlessness, feeling of exaggerated self-esteem, sometimes obsessive thoughts about the alternative purpose of the simulation design or metaverse, and intrusive thoughts about the safety and security of the user's data. Defined by a combination of two or more of the following symptoms. 1. Fear or suspicion that all interfaces in the Meta Cloud are purposely designed to steal energy from users; 2. Fear or suspicion that all interfaces in the Meta Cloud are purposely designed to control or manipulate users; 3. Fear or suspicion that all interfaces in the Meta Cloud are purposely designed to spy on users; 4. Fear or suspicion that all interfaces in the Meta Cloud are purposely designed to exploit users; 5. Fear or suspicion that all interfaces in the Meta Cloud are purposely designed to deceive users. 6. Obsessive critisizing or questioning the meaning, design, or purpose of interfaces in the Meta Cloud.

Scenario
A subject is deeply suspicious of interaction, overlook, or manipulation with some specific or multiple simulation scenarios, environments, games, aestetic or logical settings, or other users which could be considered as unreal, intrusive, or unauthorized. They are usually manifested with a feeling of anxiety, worry, or fear that the user will "lose" something important as a result of such interactions (i.e. "steal the soul" or "suck the life"). This fear may be so severe that the subject becomes completely unwilling or unable to use any interface within the Meta Cloud, even if it is necessary for their work or daily life.
